Summer Temperature Over the Years

This visualization illustrates the summer temperature distribution for the year 2021 in the Dark Canyon Wilderness area. The 3D map overlay highlights temperature variations, with a color gradient indicating different temperature ranges in degrees Celsius. The hottest regions, shown in red and orange, reach temperatures up to 26°C, while cooler areas, displayed in blue, experience temperatures as low as 21°C. This detailed spatial representation helps in understanding how temperature varies across the landscape, potentially impacting local vegetation and ecosystems. The slider at the bottom suggests that this is part of a time-series analysis, allowing users to explore temperature changes over multiple years, although only 2021 is currently displayed. This visualization is crucial for identifying trends and patterns in climate data, which can inform environmental management and conservation strategies.
